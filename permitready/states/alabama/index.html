Alabama Permit Test Overview

Minimum permit age in Alabama is 15. PermitReady also highlights important Alabama licensing rules so you can study the test format and the steps that come after you pass.

  • Question count: 30
  • Passing score: 24 correct (80%)
  • Minimum permit age: 15
  • Time limit: No official time limit listed
  • Split test: No separate scored sections listed

Study Modes

  • Practice Quiz: Untimed questions with explanations after every answer.
  • Practice Test: A state-sized simulation with 30 questions.
  • Study by Category: Focus on road signs, traffic laws, safe driving, parking, right of way, and more.
  • Review Missed Questions: Revisit mistakes quickly and learn why the correct answer is right.
  • Progress Tracking: Monitor mastery, streaks, recent scores, and study history.
  • Offline-First: Study without a constant internet connection.

State-Specific Requirements

  • Stage I learner permit begins at age 15 and requires supervised driving with a parent, guardian, instructor, or licensed driver age 21 or older in the front seat
  • Before a restricted Stage II license, the teen generally must hold the learner permit for at least 6 months and complete 50 hours of behind-the-wheel practice or an approved driver education course
  • Stage II drivers generally may not drive between midnight and 6 AM unless a listed exception or qualified adult applies
  • Stage II drivers generally may not carry more than 1 nonfamily passenger and may not use a handheld communication device while driving
  • ALEA public materials reviewed were more explicit about GDL rules than the exact scored written-test format, so the app uses a best-fit 30-question, 80% study format

Who This Is For

Great for teens preparing for a first learner's permit, parents helping a new driver study, and anyone retaking the knowledge test in Alabama.
