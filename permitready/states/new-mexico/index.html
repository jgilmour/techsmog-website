New Mexico Permit Test Overview

Minimum permit age in New Mexico is 15. PermitReady also highlights important New Mexico licensing rules so you can study the test format and the steps that come after you pass.

  • Question count: 25
  • Passing score: 18 correct (70%)
  • Minimum permit age: 15
  • Time limit: No official time limit listed
  • Split test: No separate scored sections listed

State-Specific Requirements

  • New Mexico's instructional permit begins at age 15 and requires the knowledge and vision tests, plus parental consent for applicants under 18
  • Teen applicants generally must be enrolled in an approved driver education course that includes DWI education and practice driving
  • Before a provisional license, the teen must generally hold the permit 6 months, complete 50 practice hours including 10 at night, and avoid a traffic conviction in the prior 90 days
  • New Mexico provisional drivers may not drive from midnight to 5 AM except for listed exceptions and generally may not carry more than 1 under-21 passenger who is not immediate family unless supervised by a licensed driver age 21 or older
  • Public New Mexico materials reviewed clearly state the 70% written-test passing standard but are less explicit about the scored question count for first-time teen testing, so the app uses a best-fit 25-question study format

Who This Is For

Great for teens preparing for a first learner's permit, parents helping a new driver study, and anyone retaking the knowledge test in New Mexico.
