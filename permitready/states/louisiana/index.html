Louisiana Permit Test Overview

Minimum permit age in Louisiana is 15. PermitReady also highlights important Louisiana licensing rules so you can study the test format and the steps that come after you pass.

  • Question count: 40
  • Passing score: 32 correct (80%)
  • Minimum permit age: 15
  • Time limit: No official time limit listed
  • Split test: No separate scored sections listed

State-Specific Requirements

  • Louisiana's Temporary Instructional Permit (TIP) can begin at age 14, while the learner permit itself begins at age 15
  • The learner permit knowledge test has 40 questions and requires a minimum 80% score
  • Learner permit holders generally must complete 50 supervised hours including 15 at night and may drive only with a licensed parent, guardian, qualifying adult age 21 or older, or licensed sibling age 18 or older
  • Learner permits generally must be held 180 days before the intermediate license at age 16
  • Louisiana intermediate drivers face 11 PM to 5 AM curfew limits, a 1 under-21 non-family passenger rule during evening and overnight hours, and a no-cell-phone rule for younger drivers

Who This Is For

Great for teens preparing for a first learner's permit, parents helping a new driver study, and anyone retaking the knowledge test in Louisiana.
