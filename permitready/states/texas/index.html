Texas DPS Permit Test Practice

PermitReady is a free iOS app that helps teens prepare for the Texas DPS permit test. The exam has 30 questions; you need at least 21 correct (70%) to pass. The official test is timed at 60 minutes. The official test includes separate scored sections, so make sure you practice both sign recognition and road-rule questions. Texas Permit Test Overview

Minimum permit age in Texas is 15. PermitReady also highlights important Texas licensing rules so you can study the test format and the steps that come after you pass.

  • Question count: 30
  • Passing score: 21 correct (70%)
  • Minimum permit age: 15
  • Time limit: 60 minutes
  • Split test: Yes, separate scored sections apply

Study Modes

  • Practice Quiz: Untimed questions with explanations after every answer.
  • Practice Test: A state-sized simulation with 30 questions and a 60-minute timer.
  • Study by Category: Focus on road signs, traffic laws, safe driving, parking, right of way, and more.
  • Review Missed Questions: Revisit mistakes quickly and learn why the correct answer is right.
  • Progress Tracking: Monitor mastery, streaks, recent scores, and study history.
  • Offline-First: Study without a constant internet connection.

State-Specific Requirements

  • The test is strictly split into two sections: Highway Signs (15 questions) and Traffic Laws (15 questions)
  • You must score at least 70% to pass the exam
  • Minors (under 21) face Zero Tolerance laws: Any detectable amount of alcohol results in license suspension
  • Provisional license holders (under 18) cannot drive between midnight and 5:00 AM
  • Provisional license holders cannot have more than one passenger under 21 who is not a family member

Who This Is For

Great for teens preparing for a first learner's permit, parents helping a new driver study, and anyone retaking the knowledge test in Texas.
