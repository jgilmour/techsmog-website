Mississippi Permit Test Overview

Minimum permit age in Mississippi is 15. PermitReady also highlights important Mississippi licensing rules so you can study the test format and the steps that come after you pass.

  • Question count: 30
  • Passing score: 24 correct (80%)
  • Minimum permit age: 15
  • Time limit: No official time limit listed
  • Split test: No separate scored sections listed

Study Modes

State-Specific Requirements

  • Mississippi's regular learner permit begins at age 15 and requires proof of school enrollment or other qualifying school-status documentation for most applicants under 18
  • Applicants must pass both the knowledge and eye exam, and the regular learner permit is generally valid for 2 years
  • A 16-year-old generally must hold the learner permit for 12 months or until the 17th birthday before qualifying for a regular Class R license
  • Applicants age 17 and older may obtain the learner permit and driver license on the same day after passing the knowledge and eye exam and presenting required documents
  • Mississippi public materials reviewed were much clearer about permit-age, school-status, and holding-period rules than the exact scored question count, so the app uses a best-fit 30-question, 80% study format

Who This Is For

Great for teens preparing for a first learner's permit, parents helping a new driver study, and anyone retaking the knowledge test in Mississippi.
