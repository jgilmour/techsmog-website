Kentucky Permit Test Overview

Minimum permit age in Kentucky is 15. PermitReady also highlights important Kentucky licensing rules so you can study the test format and the steps that come after you pass.

  • Question count: 40
  • Passing score: 32 correct (80%)
  • Minimum permit age: 15
  • Time limit: No official time limit listed
  • Split test: No separate scored sections listed

Study Modes

State-Specific Requirements

  • Kentucky teen permit applicants may begin at age 15 and must pass written and vision tests through Kentucky State Police
  • Kentucky's written test is officially scored at 80%; public KSP materials indicate the operator permit exam uses 40 questions
  • Permit holders age 15 through 20 generally must hold the permit for 180 days before road testing
  • Before the intermediate license, the teen must complete a 60-hour practice log including 10 hours at night
  • Permit holders must drive with a licensed driver age 21 or older in the passenger seat, and public-school-age applicants 15 to 17 are also subject to Kentucky's school-compliance rule

Who This Is For

Great for teens preparing for a first learner's permit, parents helping a new driver study, and anyone retaking the knowledge test in Kentucky.
