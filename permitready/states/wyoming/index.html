Wyoming Permit Test Overview

Minimum permit age in Wyoming is 15. PermitReady also highlights important Wyoming licensing rules so you can study the test format and the steps that come after you pass.

  • Question count: 25
  • Passing score: 20 correct (80%)
  • Minimum permit age: 15
  • Time limit: No official time limit listed
  • Split test: No separate scored sections listed

State-Specific Requirements

  • Wyoming's regular learner permit is generally issued at ages 15 to 16, requires a written exam and vision screening, and is valid for 1 year
  • Permit holders generally must drive with a licensed person over age 18 in the front passenger seat, and the permit must be held at least 10 days before applying for broader driving privileges
  • For the intermediate permit at age 16, Wyoming generally requires 50 actual behind-the-wheel hours including 10 at night plus a driving skills test
  • Wyoming's intermediate permit generally allows driving only from 5 AM to 11 PM, bars more than 1 passenger under 18 who is not immediate family, and requires all occupants to wear seat belts unless a qualifying 18+ fully licensed driver is in the front seat
  • WYDOT public materials reviewed were clearer about graduated-license ages, hours, and restrictions than the exact scored Class C written-test question count, so the app uses a best-fit 25-question, 80% study format

Who This Is For

Great for teens preparing for a first learner's permit, parents helping a new driver study, and anyone retaking the knowledge test in Wyoming.
