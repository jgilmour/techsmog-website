North Dakota Permit Test Overview

Minimum permit age in North Dakota is 14. PermitReady also highlights important North Dakota licensing rules so you can study the test format and the steps that come after you pass.

  • Question count: 25
  • Passing score: 20 correct (80%)
  • Minimum permit age: 14
  • Time limit: No official time limit listed
  • Split test: No separate scored sections listed

State-Specific Requirements

  • North Dakota's learner permit begins at age 14 and requires the knowledge test, vision screen, and a sponsor's signature for applicants under 18
  • Permit holders ages 14-15 generally must hold the permit 12 months or until age 16, but at least 6 months; ages 16-17 generally must hold it at least 6 months or until age 18
  • Drivers under 16 generally must complete 50 supervised practice hours in variable conditions before licensing
  • North Dakota residents can qualify for a restricted license at age 15, and that license generally bars driving from sunset or 9 PM, whichever is later, until 5 AM except for listed reasons
  • Drivers under 18 in North Dakota may not use electronic communication devices while driving, including while operating on a learner permit

Who This Is For
