Oklahoma Permit Test Overview

Minimum permit age in Oklahoma is 15. PermitReady also highlights important Oklahoma licensing rules so you can study the test format and the steps that come after you pass.

  • Question count: 20
  • Passing score: 15 correct (75%)
  • Minimum permit age: 15
  • Time limit: No official time limit listed
  • Split test: No separate scored sections listed

State-Specific Requirements

  • Oklahoma's written exam is officially scored at 15 correct answers out of 20 questions
  • A learner permit generally begins at age 15 for applicants enrolled in approved driver education, or at age 16 without driver education
  • Learner permit holders generally may drive from 5 AM to 10 PM with a licensed driver age 21 or older and must hold the permit at least 180 days before the intermediate step
  • Before the drive test, teens generally must complete 50 behind-the-wheel hours including 10 at night, have no traffic convictions, and complete the Work Zone Safe online course
  • Oklahoma's under-18 graduated-license restriction generally allows driving from 5 AM to 10 PM, limits the teen to 1 passenger or household passengers, and requires a licensed driver age 21 or older in the front seat for broader exceptions

Who This Is For

Great for teens preparing for a first learner's permit, parents helping a new driver study, and anyone retaking the knowledge test in Oklahoma.
