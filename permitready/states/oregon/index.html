Oregon Permit Test Overview

Minimum permit age in Oregon is 15. PermitReady also highlights important Oregon licensing rules so you can study the test format and the steps that come after you pass.

  • Question count: 35
  • Passing score: 28 correct (80%)
  • Minimum permit age: 15
  • Time limit: No official time limit listed
  • Split test: No separate scored sections listed

State-Specific Requirements

  • Oregon officially says the Class C knowledge test has 35 multiple-choice questions and requires 28 correct answers to pass
  • Applicants under 18 generally must hold the instruction permit for at least 6 months, and out-of-state permit time can count
  • Teens under 18 generally need 100 supervised hours without approved driver education or 50 supervised hours with an ODOT-approved course certificate
  • For the first year of provisional licensing, Oregon generally restricts driving between midnight and 5 AM except for listed exceptions and limits passengers under 20 in two stages
  • Oregon also makes mobile-device use unlawful while operating a motor vehicle, including talking or texting on a cell phone even with a hands-free accessory

Who This Is For

Great for teens preparing for a first learner's permit, parents helping a new driver study, and anyone retaking the knowledge test in Oregon.
