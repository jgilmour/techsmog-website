Idaho Permit Test Overview

Minimum permit age in Idaho is 14.5. PermitReady also highlights important Idaho licensing rules so you can study the test format and the steps that come after you pass.

  • Question count: 40
  • Passing score: 34 correct (85%)
  • Minimum permit age: 14.5
  • Time limit: No official time limit listed
  • Split test: No separate scored sections listed

State-Specific Requirements

  • Driver Training Permit begins at age 14 years and 6 months for applicants under 17; a Class D instruction permit is also available for applicants age 17 or older
  • Idaho's Class D knowledge test has 40 questions and allows up to 6 missed answers, which means 34 correct to pass
  • Drivers under 17 generally must complete a violation-free 6-month supervised instruction period with 50 hours of practice including 10 at night
  • During the supervised period, a licensed driver age 21 or older must sit in the front seat and all occupants must wear seat belts
  • Drivers under 16 are limited to daylight-only travel unless accompanied by a licensed driver age 21 or older, and newly licensed drivers under 17 face a 1-passenger-under-17 limit during the first 6 months

Who This Is For

Great for teens preparing for a first learner's permit, parents helping a new driver study, and anyone retaking the knowledge test in Idaho.
