Utah Permit Test Overview

Minimum permit age in Utah is 15. PermitReady also highlights important Utah licensing rules so you can study the test format and the steps that come after you pass.

  • Question count: 50
  • Passing score: 40 correct (80%)
  • Minimum permit age: 15
  • Time limit: No official time limit listed

State-Specific Requirements

  • Utah's learner permit begins at age 15 and requires passing a 50-question closed-book written knowledge test
  • Utah's 2026 driver handbook says the written knowledge test typically takes 30 to 45 minutes and requires a score of 80% or better, while learner-permit scores and permits remain valid for 18 months
  • For teen first-time licensing, Utah generally requires the permit to be held 6 months and until age 16, plus driver education, 40 practice hours with 10 after sunset, and the Traffic Safety and Trends Exam
  • Newly licensed Utah drivers ages 16-17 may not drive between midnight and 5 AM except for listed exceptions and generally may carry only immediate family members during the first 6 months or until age 18
  • Utah allows the permit-holding period to count from an out-of-state learner permit and permits several types of qualified front-seat supervisors, including a licensed parent, guardian, approved instructor, responsible adult signer, or an authorized adult age 21 or older
