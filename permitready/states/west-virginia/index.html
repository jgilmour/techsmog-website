West Virginia Permit Test Overview

Minimum permit age in West Virginia is 15. PermitReady also highlights important West Virginia licensing rules so you can study the test format and the steps that come after you pass.

State-Specific Requirements

  • West Virginia's knowledge examination has at least 25 questions, and applicants must answer 19 correctly to pass
  • The Level 1 GDL instruction permit begins at age 15 and requires a vision screening, knowledge test, school eligibility certificate, and parental or guardian consent for most minors
  • Level 1 permit holders may drive only from 5 AM to 10 PM with a licensed driver age 21 or older in the right front seat and may have only 2 additional non-family passengers
  • Before the Level 2 GDL intermediate license, West Virginia generally requires 180 consecutive conviction-free days at Level 1 plus either 50 behind-the-wheel hours including 10 at night or an approved driver education course
  • During the first 6 months of Level 2, no non-family passengers under 20 are allowed; during the second 6 months, only 1 non-family passenger under 20 is allowed, and nighttime driving from 10 PM to 5 AM generally requires a qualifying exception or supervising adult
