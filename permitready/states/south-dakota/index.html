South Dakota Permit Test Overview

Minimum permit age in South Dakota is 14. PermitReady also highlights important South Dakota licensing rules so you can study the test format and the steps that come after you pass.

  • Question count: 25
  • Passing score: 20 correct (80%)
  • Minimum permit age: 14
  • Time limit: No official time limit listed
  • Split test: No separate scored sections listed

State-Specific Requirements

  • South Dakota's instruction permit begins at age 14 and generally requires the knowledge test unless a qualifying state-approved driver education course waives that testing step
  • Teens generally must hold the instruction permit 275 days without driver education or 180 days with approved driver education before the restricted step
  • Before a restricted permit, South Dakota generally requires 50 parent- or guardian-supervised hours including 10 at night and 10 in inclement weather
  • The restricted permit generally allows solo driving from 6 AM to 10 PM, requires a legal guardian for 10 PM to 6 AM driving, and uses staged passenger limits outside the immediate family or household
  • South Dakota officially states the knowledge test must be passed with a score of 80% or higher; public materials reviewed were less explicit about the scored question count, so the app uses a best-fit 25-question study format

Who This Is For

Great for teens preparing for a first learner's permit, parents helping a new driver study, and anyone retaking the knowledge test in South Dakota.
