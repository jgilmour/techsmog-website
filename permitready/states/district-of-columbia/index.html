Washington, DC Permit Test Overview

Minimum permit age in Washington, DC is 16. PermitReady also highlights important Washington, DC licensing rules so you can study the test format and the steps that come after you pass.

  • Question count: 30
  • Passing score: 24 correct (80%)
  • Minimum permit age: 16
  • Time limit: 75 minutes
  • Split test: No separate scored sections listed

State-Specific Requirements

  • DC learner permit available at age 16 and generally valid for 2 years
  • For teen applicants in the GRAD program, the official practice test describes a 30-question knowledge test with a 75-minute limit
  • Permit driving is limited to 6 AM through 9 PM
  • Before a provisional license, the learner generally must hold the permit 6 months and complete a 40-hour certification log with a qualified adult age 21 or older
  • The exact GRAD passing threshold is less explicit on DC public pages reviewed, so the app uses the widely cited 80% standard (24 of 30)

Who This Is For

Great for teens preparing for a first learner's permit, parents helping a new driver study, and anyone retaking the knowledge test in Washington, DC.
